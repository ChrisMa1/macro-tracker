about & goal setting ▼
This app tracks protein density — how much protein you're getting per calorie eaten. The chart plots your food intake in real time. Log workouts to pull the dot down, reflecting a lower net intake. The goal is to stay to the right of the diagonal line: high protein, controlled calories.
cal Net calorie goal
This is your target for food calories minus workout burns. Set it based on your goal relative to your RMR:
  • Fat loss — set 300–500 kcal below your RMR. A 500 kcal daily deficit yields roughly 1 lb / 0.45 kg of loss per week. Avoid going below 1,200 kcal (women) or 1,500 kcal (men) net.
  • Maintenance — set equal to your RMR.
  • Muscle gain — set 200–300 kcal above your RMR. Larger surpluses tend to add fat faster than muscle.
On days you work out, your food budget effectively increases by the calories burned — eat more, hit the same net.
pro Protein goal
Base it on your bodyweight and how hard you train:
  • Sedentary / general health — 0.36 g per lb (0.8 g/kg).
  • Active / recomposition — 0.7–0.9 g per lb (1.6–2.0 g/kg). Sweet spot for most people training 3–5× per week.
  • Aggressive cut or heavy training — up to 1.0 g per lb (2.2 g/kg) to preserve muscle in a deficit.
Example: 165 lb person training 4× per week → 165 × 0.8 = 132 g/day.
rmr Resting metabolic rate
RMR is your sedentary daily burn — no exercise included. Workouts are logged separately and widen your deficit automatically. Use BMR × 1.2 as a starting point (accounts for basic daily movement). Treat it as an estimate and adjust based on real-world weight changes over 2–3 weeks.

chart Reading the chart
X axis = protein eaten. Y axis = net calories (food − workout burns). Three reference lines:
  • Diagonal (purple) — your protein density target. Staying to the right means your food choices are protein-efficient enough to hit your protein goal within your calorie budget.
  • Vertical (green) — your protein goal. Cross it to hit your daily target.
  • Horizontal (peach) — your net calorie goal. Stay near or below it.
The ideal position is near the intersection of all three lines — the hollow target dot. Trail dots show how your position evolved throughout the day. Tap any dot to see what you logged.
All calculations are estimates. Individual metabolic rates vary. Consult a registered dietitian or physician before making significant dietary changes.
